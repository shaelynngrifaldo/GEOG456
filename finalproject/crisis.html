The U.S. Opioid Crisis

What is the Opioid Crisis?

The US opioid crisis is a public health emergency that is characterized by the misuse and addiction to opioid drugs, including prescription painfillers, heroin, and sythetic opiods.

This is an increasinging threaten to society with a staggering number of overdoses, deaths, and devasting societal impacts. This crisis is due to various factors, including overprescribing of opioid medications by heatlhcare providers, hyperactive marketing by pharmaceutical companies, and multiple socioeconomic factors like poverty and unemployment.

CURRENT NEWS!

April 03, 2024 The White House released a statement that the Republican Study Committee, which represents a significant portion of the House Republications, proposed a budget that would reduce a funding for states to combat the opioid epidemic; despite opioids being the leading cause of death among 19 to 49 year-olds in America.

The Republican's proposed budget plan threatens to cut funding by over 30% including slashing funding for State Opioid Response Grants- which supports essential services like medication-assisted treatment and overdose reversal efforts.

This proposed budget plan jeopardizes the progress made in providing treatment and support to millions of Americans with substance use disorders.

Prescription Opioids

Prescription opioids can be used to treat moderate-to-severe pain and are often prescribed after surgery or injury, or for cancer.

The most commonly found prescribed opioids are:

Hydrocodone

Oxycodone

Oxymorphone

Morphine

Codeine

Fentanyl

Hydromorphone

Tapentadol

Methadone

The Threats of Prescribing Opioids

Opioids can be very addictive and causes risks of misuse, addiction, and overdose

Opioids affect the part of the brain that is in control of breathing. Taking to high of a dose can slow you breathing and cause death.

Combining opioids with alcohol and other drugs can increase the chance of death.
