West Virginia Case Study Results

The data represented in this analysis offers insights on the relationship between opioid deaths and opioid prescriptions in West Virginia throughout 2013 to 2020. The consistent downward trend in the opioid death rates mirrors that of the prescription totals of opioids.

This correlation suggests that there is a potential association between prescription dispensing and opioid-related deaths. The declining prescription opioid totals may indicate success in the overall efforts to stop overprescriptions and limit access to opioids, which may contirbute the decline in opioid-related deaths.

Socio-Economic Limitations

Despite the potential to having a correlation between the two variables, further analysis is necessary to establish a more causal relationship between the two. While the correlation between opioid death rates and prescriptions is present in West Virginia, the reality of the relationship is dependent situation-to-situation and state-to-state.

Socioeconomic condtions like access to healthcare, availability of addiction treatment services, and changes in drug supply all have the possibilty of playing a signifiacnt role in an individuals opioid use.
